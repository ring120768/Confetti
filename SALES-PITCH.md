## The one-liner

**The wedding planner you didn't think you could afford, in your pocket.**

## The elevator pitch

A professional wedding planner costs £2,000–£6,000 — so 9 out of 10 UK couples plan alone, juggling spreadsheets, screenshots and 3am panic lists. Wedding Planner Pro gives every couple what only the lucky few had: a planner who knows *your* wedding — your date, budget, style and progress — tells you exactly what to do and when, researches venues and suppliers for you, and even drafts the enquiry emails. From free, to £18.99 a month for the full monty.

## Meet Buzz 🐝 (the star of the pitch)

Not a chatbot bolted onto a checklist — a planner with your file open:

- **Knows your wedding.** Ask "what should we be doing this month?" and Buzz answers from *your* actual plan — your dates, your overdue tasks, your budget.
- **Does real research.** "Find us barn venues near Canterbury for 80 guests under £8k" → a live-researched shortlist favouring venues rated 4★+, with ratings, sources and links. No made-up suggestions — everything cited.
- **Takes action.** Love a venue? Buzz saves it to your supplier pipeline — with the venue's real contact details — and drafts the availability enquiry with your date, numbers and the questions a pro would ask. You send it from your own email; replies come to you.
- **Talks like a friend who's planned 100 weddings.** Warm, honest, British. Knows what flowers cost in Kent and what to ask a videographer.

## The plan that plans itself

- Enter your date → a complete UK wedding plan appears, **every task auto-scheduled** by professional lead times (order the dress ~12 months out, give legal notice by 29 days, chase RSVPs at 8 weeks…).
- **140 expert tasks** with plain-English guidance, typical UK costs, and questions-to-ask-suppliers on every booking task.
- A warm **journey timeline** — Just Engaged → Big Decisions → Details → Final Countdown → The Day — not a scary project chart. Progress you can feel.
- **UK legal accuracy built in**: register office notice rules, CofE banns, registrar booking.
- **Marrying abroad?** Full destination module: CNI and apostille paperwork, statutory declarations for Commonwealth countries, local planner tasks, guest travel — the stuff even planning books skip.
- Tick a task and confetti flies. Weddings should feel like weddings.

## Everyday superpowers

- **Guest list & RSVPs** — shareable RSVP link, dietary requirements collected automatically.
- **Budget tracking** with realistic UK benchmarks (the average UK wedding is ~£20,600 — we help you beat it, or spend it well).
- **Supplier pipeline** — researching → enquired → quoted → booked, with quotes and contacts in one place.
- **Plan together** — invite your partner and helpers; assign tasks to the best man.
- **Works everywhere, installs in seconds** — phone, tablet, desktop from one link. No app store, no download queue. Add to home screen and it's an app.

## Why us and not… (objection handling)

- **"Bridebook is free."** Bridebook is a brilliant directory with tools. We're a *planner*. Directories wait for you to search; a planner tells you what's next, chases what's late, and does the legwork. Try asking a directory to draft your venue email.
- **"ChatGPT can plan my wedding."** It can chat about weddings. It doesn't know yours — no task list, no budget, no RSVPs, no memory of what's booked. Buzz answers from your actual plan and updates it.
- **"We'll just use a spreadsheet."** So did everyone who forgot to give legal notice by the 29-day deadline. The plan that plans itself doesn't forget.
- **"Is our data safe?"** UK-hosted database, bank-grade row-level security — only you and your partner can see your wedding. Delete everything, any time.
